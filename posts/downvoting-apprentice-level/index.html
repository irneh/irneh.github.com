Downvote me like a Boss

May 14, 2013

I love being wrong because it means I learned something. me, as often as possible

I wrote a piece about JavaScript being accidentally bringing F/OSS into the Enterprise. The piece got steady upvotes. Then came comments. The comments were about Linux and such, not about JavaScript. Once the comments came the downvotes came as well. Were the downvotes because Windows folks were reacting to the comments made by Linux folks? We’ll never know because the downvotes came without comment.

I wrote a piece describing a feeling about photography. I posted it and was promptly schooled on Reddit. The comment was polite but it struck me because I realized I hadn’t thought things through and, well, it showed I really didn’t know what the heck I was talking about. So thank you. Now I’m better educated, in a better position to help others and won’t noisy up your neighboorhood with my silly ideas.

Writing forces you to work ideas through. Up and downvoting is a reaction, like rolling up the car window if you don’t like the noise from the car next to you. That’s fine but it doesn’t make me go away any faster than ignoring me.

Downvoting Samples

You don’t like this post? Here is a template to respond with:

We already have guidlines: http://ycombinator.com/newsguidelines.html. This is a tired subject. Please stop beating the dead horse.

Or:

We are not here for your education. Please buy a book or go back to school. blogspam

If you can go one better, work your thought through and give us one true sentence, profane or not.

Please, downvote me to hell but give us something to read along the way.
